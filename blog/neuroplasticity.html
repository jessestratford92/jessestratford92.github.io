NEUROPLASTICITY

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity is an umbrella term that is used to refer to the brain's ability of modifying, changing and adaptability in both structure and function throughout one's life. Understanding these principles allows oneself to graduate from previous learning or thought patterns - to allow oneself to achieve a new mindset that could be more beneficial to a person's mental health/growth and physical understanding of one's nature. In many cases we are told that we ‘inherit’ brain function, and that a lot is unachievable if you have a brain that works a certain way that might not fit the normality of certain learning environments. What understanding neuroplasticity is - is that our brains can be molded like plastic, it breaks down classical learning models and enables us to learn new pathways or expand parts of your brain to achieve better functionality and performance.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

A way in which to increase your neuroplasticity is to switch your mindset, switch your learning style, and find a way that specifically works for you. This can be ways like working in more of a community based style of learning. Making learning not only a ‘self’ based system, but a system where each all learns with one another, and goals are set as a team. This enables support and understanding for one another and promotes an overall more healthy way to look at difficult tasks or learning obstacles.

This is so relevant in our current day and age where learning models are only seeing a slow change in our overall system of education.
